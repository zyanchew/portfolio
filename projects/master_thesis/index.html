Master's Graduation Project

üìçTitle: Synthesizing Social Networks for Travel Behavior Models

This research explores the use of Bayesian Belief Networks (BBNs) to synthesize social networks and it’s strength for further integration into travel behavior models. The study focuses on understanding how socio-demographic factors and social relationships influence joint activity participation.

üí° Key Findings

  • Individuals with larger networks of friends are more likely to engage in activities such as dining out.
  • Those with more siblings in their networks tend to take family vacations more often.
  • People are more likely to visit or host friends compared to colleagues.

These findings highlight the importance of both the size and the nature of social relationships in shaping mobility behavior.

üîç Methodology

Bayesian Belief Networks (BBNs) are employed to model the interdependencies between social networks and travel behavior. The analysis uses GeNIe software to reason probabilistically and infer relationships between multiple variables.

The conceptual framework for this research project:

  • Model I synthesizes individuals’ socio-demographic characteristics, social network composition, and joint activity frequencies at the ego level.

  • Model II emphasizes a disaggregated view of social interactions, incorporating ego’s and alter’s socio-demographic characteristics, homophily/heterogeneity between ego and alter, social relationship types, and joint activity frequency.

üìå Conclusions

  • Demonstrates the feasibility of using BBNs for synthetic social network generation.
  • Addresses data limitations and privacy concerns in modeling sensitive social data.
  • Offers an approach to enhance travel behavior prediction models by integrating social dimensions.
